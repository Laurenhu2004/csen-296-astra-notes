Assignment Title
Week 5.2 Lab: AstraNotes Requirements-to-UML Traceability Matrix

Instructions
For this lab, I want you to verify whether your AstraNotes UML package actually supports the important requirements you defined earlier in the quarter.

This week is about validation, not expansion. I do not want five new diagrams. I want you to test whether your current design is traceable, coherent, and ready to support implementation planning.

What To Submit
Submit one PDF or one Text Entry containing:

a simple requirements-to-UML traceability matrix
a short traceability metrics summary
a brief gap analysis
a short note explaining how AI helped you build or refine the matrix
Your Traceability Matrix Must Include
For each important requirement, include:

Requirement ID
Requirement
Class/Object Evidence
Use Case/Activity Evidence
Deployment Evidence
Status
Gap Note
Status Labels To Use
Use one of these for each requirement:

Fully Traced
Partially Traced
Weakly Traced
Not Traced
Recommended Workflow

select the 5 to 8 most important AstraNotes requirements
assign stable IDs such as FR-1, FR-2, or NFR-1
reuse the wording from your refined requirement baseline
identify structural evidence in the class or object diagrams
identify behavioral evidence in the use case or activity diagrams
identify deployment evidence when relevant
assign a traceability status
record the gap or inconsistency in one short sentence
summarize the metrics
write a brief gap analysis explaining what should be refined before implementation
Traceability Metrics To Report
At minimum, report:

total requirements reviewed
number Fully Traced
number Partially Traced
number Weakly Traced
number Not Traced
number of major UML elements that do not have a clear requirement reason to exist
AI Use Guidance
You may use AI tools such as GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT, or Lucid AI to help draft IDs, normalize wording, or suggest an initial matrix structure. However, I want the final mapping, status labels, metrics, and gap analysis to reflect your judgment.

What I Am Looking For
I am looking for:

evidence-based mapping rather than guesswork
honest recognition of weak or missing support
consistent naming across requirements and UML artifacts
awareness of overdesigned elements that have no clear requirement reason to exist
thoughtful use of AI as a helper rather than an unquestioned source
Reminder
Every important requirement should have visible design evidence, and every major design element should have a requirement reason to exist.
